Potential for Investment Attraction Index
Região Centro - Portugal

We're always thinking, studying and analysing diverse ways to validate an investment location. Have you ever thought investing in Portugal? More: have you ever thought about the potential for investment attraction in certains regions of Portugal?

At this interactive plataform we're presenting the results of a Master's Degree dissertation, the Potential for Investiment Attraction Index (PIIA) for the 100 cities of "Região Centro" in Portugal (NUTS II). The index had, as objective, to qualify in a comprehensible way, the potential of each municipality, by using consolidated scientific methodologies at economic and territorial studies, as well as gain public attention for the ones with low performances and their needings for more effective policies. The Index is not considering any environmental or turism varieble, wanting to qualify the potential, mainly, for the urban enviroment at the cities.

The comprehension of the main territorial characteristics is a fundamental step to finally put planning in action and this study aims, also, to give an idea of different cities potentials for the general public and raise debates about cities, their relations and the index results. The Index variables were choosen under a strong review of economic and investment studies and their models to select the best places to qualify cities, areas within a city or even a whole region and finally, invest.

Thus, several factors were synthesized, presenting a brief qualification of the economic, social, demographic, competitive situation for each city at the region. The final scores were ranked in a decreasing ranking, where the first placed have greater potential and the latter, less.

According to Centro2020 Program, up to the present year 2020, more than 8 thousand projects have been approved, corresponding to 2,7 billion euros in the last four years. The data were revealed by the Coordination and Development Commision for Região Centro (CCDRC). These data show us the growing and important approval of projects for development which, consequently, benefits society as a whole.

The first 20 cities at the Potential for Investiment Attraction Index have, in general, a good socioeconomic structure (lower unemployment rates, median monthly family incomes and good purchasing power) - usually the first ones to be analyzed in investment studies for the private sector - and average performances in analyzes of business competitiviness and territorial infrastructures. The last 20 showed low performances especially in the analysis of the infrastructures available in the territory, which results in less cohesion.

We invite you to take a look at the final and simplified results of this study. Questions, suggestions and any contact with the author are welcome.

INDICATORS

For a better comprehension of the reality for this region, the selection of variables was made identifying the main elements that make a region attractive for the implementation of development strategies by the public sector, in partnership with private agents. It's important to notice that the studies used as bases for the present Index vary in the granularity of the analysis: some of them aimed to qualify a region whitin a city, some to qualify cities at all and other ones a whole region.

Thus, considering the regularity of different variables in different studies, the variable selection was made. 20 variables where choosen and then, grouped into 3 dimensions of the Index: Socioeconomic, Competitive Scenario and Territorial Infrastructures and Accessibility. You can check a better defition for each dimension at "Methodology" section. The variables are listed below:

  • Population Density
  • Potential Sustainability Index
  • Migratory Balance
  • Unemployment Rate

  • Doctors / 1000 inhab

  Competitive Scenario

  • Business Density
  • Business Survival Rate
  • Business Balance
  • Apparent Productivity
  • Companies / 100 inhab
  • Business Incubators
  • Science and Technology Parks

METHODOLOGIC NOTES

Index Dimensions

Here are the dimensions defitions:

  • Socioeconomic: aims to group variables that reflects the city's reality at demographic and economic statistics;
  • Competitive Scenario: the objective of this dimension is to present the business reality of each city, using statistics related to business density, survival rates, existance of support infrastructures as tech parks and incubators;
  • Territorial Infrastructures and Accessibility: being the most "geographic" dimension, it shows some measures of artificial territory (anthropized areas), zoned areas, as well as the within city accessibility and its distance travels to the main economic poles of the country.

Scoring and Calculations

The Potential for Investment Atraction Index - Região Centro was calculated after a Principal Components Analysis (PCA), combining the pontuation of each city on each index dimension in a weighted equation, using the weights of the final PCA. For this Index, only the 100 cities of Região Centro were analyzed.
The scores presented at the table, however, are scaled between 1 and 100, using each column values. Having a 100 score does not means that the city is perfect, it shows only that it has the greatest pontuation between the analyzed cities. The scoring formula was:
Score: 99 * (((x(i) - x(min)) / ((x(max) - x(min))) + 1
This re-scaling was done only for this web version of the index and not at the original data of the dissertation.

Quintile Classification

Only for the map representation (and a better qualification for each city), the results were classified in quintiles with 20 cities, each class has a potential qualification, going from "Very Low" (red cities) to "Very High" (greener cities) in Potential for Investment Attraction.

SOURCES

At the Socioeconomic dimension, all data was obtained at National Statistics Institute (INE), by PORDATA website, all of date from 2018.

At the Competitive Scenario dimension, the variables "Purchasing Power", "Business Survival Rate" and "Business Balance" were also obtained at INE, but date from 2017. "Business Density" and "Companies / 100 inhab" date from 2018. The variables "Science and Technology Parks" and "Business Incubators" are binary (1 - there's at least one, 0 - there's no one) and were obtained at Inovation National Agency (2016) and at Incubators National Network (2020).

At the Territorial Infrastructures and Accessibility dimension, the main sources were the Official Occupation and Land Use Cartography for Portugal - COS2018, developed by DGT and OpenStreetMap data. For the Municipal Accessibility, a script was made to calculate the area reached within the city in a 10 minute travel by car from each city hall, the area was inverse weighted by the city's area to create a score. The distances to Lisbon and Porto are represented in hours of travel, the routes were made with OSM data.
